A Timeline of Artificial Intelligence

  • 1950 - Turing Test: Alan Turing proposes the Turing Test as a measure of machine intelligence.
  • 1956 - Dartmouth Conference: The term "Artificial Intelligence" is coined at the Dartmouth Conference.
  • 1960s - Early AI Research: Development of programs like ELIZA and SHRDLU.
  • 1974-1980 - First AI Winter: A period of reduced funding and interest in AI research.
  • 1980s - Expert Systems: Rise of expert systems, marking a return of interest in AI.
  • 1987-1993 - Second AI Winter: Another period of stagnation in AI.
  • 1997 - Deep Blue Victory: IBM's Deep Blue defeats world chess champion Garry Kasparov.
  • 2000s - Machine Learning & Data: Focus on machine learning and the availability of large data sets.
  • 2012 - Deep Learning Breakthrough: Success of deep learning models in the ImageNet competition.
  • 2016 - AlphaGo's Victory: Google DeepMind's AlphaGo defeats world champion Go player Lee Sedol.
  • 2020s - GPT-3 and Beyond: OpenAI introduces GPT-3, a significant advancement in language processing.
  • Ongoing - Ethical and Societal Implications: Increasing focus on the ethical and societal implications of AI.
